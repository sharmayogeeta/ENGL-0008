üß† What is Prewriting?

Definition:

Prewriting is the thinking and writing you do before you begin an assignment to help you generate ideas.

Why is Prewriting Important?

  • üéØ Helps you focus your thoughts
  • üí° Generates creative ideas
  • üìã Organizes your thinking
  • ‚è∞ Saves time when writing
  • üìà Improves essay quality

Three Main Prewriting Techniques:

1. Freewriting: Write continuously without stopping
2. Brainstorming: List ideas quickly
3. Mind Mapping: Create visual connections between ideas

‚úçÔ∏è Freewriting Technique

How to Freewrite:

  1. Set a timer for 5-10 minutes
  2. Write continuously about your topic
  3. Don't worry about grammar, spelling, or organization
  4. Don't stop writing - keep your pen moving
  5. If you get stuck, write "I'm stuck" until new ideas come
Freewriting Example: "Online vs. Traditional Education"

"I'm thinking about online education versus traditional classroom learning. Online is more flexible, I can study at home in my pajamas, don't need to commute. But traditional classes have more interaction, can ask questions immediately, meet classmates face to face. Online requires more self-discipline, traditional has set schedule that forces me to attend. Technology issues with online, but traditional classes might be canceled for weather..."

Remember: Freewriting is about getting ideas out, not perfect writing!

üí≠ Brainstorming Technique

How to Brainstorm:

  1. Write your topic at the top of the page
  2. List ALL ideas that come to mind
  3. Don't judge ideas as "good" or "bad"
  4. Write quickly - quantity over quality
  5. Use single words or short phrases
Brainstorming Example: "Working from Home vs. Office"

Working from Home:

  • No commute
  • Flexible hours
  • Comfortable clothes
  • Own food
  • Quiet environment
  • Need self-discipline
  • Isolated

Office Work:

  • Team collaboration
  • Professional atmosphere
  • Set schedule
  • Office equipment
  • Commute stress
  • Dress code
  • Social interaction

‚ö†Ô∏è Understanding Sentence Fragments

What is a Sentence Fragment?

A sentence fragment is an incomplete sentence that is missing either a subject, a verb, or doesn't express a complete thought.

Complete Sentence Requirements:

1. Subject: Who or what the sentence is about
2. Verb: What the subject does or is
3. Complete thought: Makes sense by itself
Examples:

‚ùå Fragments:

  • "Running in the park." (no subject)
  • "The student with the book." (no verb)
  • "When I go to school." (incomplete thought)

‚úÖ Complete Sentences:

  • "I am running in the park."
  • "The student with the book is reading."
  • "When I go to school, I meet my friends."
11

üîß How to Fix Sentence Fragments

Three Ways to Fix Fragments:

1. Add Missing Subject or Verb

Fragment: "Working late every night."
Fixed: "She is working late every night."

2. Connect to Another Sentence

Fragment: "Because it was raining."
Fixed: "We stayed inside because it was raining."

3. Complete the Thought

Fragment: "The book that I borrowed."
Fixed: "The book that I borrowed is very interesting."

Practice: Can you fix these fragments?
  1. "Studying for the exam."
  2. "The teacher in the classroom."
  3. "After we finish dinner."

üë• Peer Assessment Guidelines

Evaluating Unit 2 Written Assignments:

You'll assess your classmates' compare/contrast paragraphs using these criteria:

Remember: Give constructive feedback and grade fairly (1-10 scale). Your goal is to help your classmates improve!
